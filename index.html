3 Start Simple: The MVP

Before building the full Live Activity suite, there's a version we can ship fast that solves the most acute user pain — and proves the concept with real data. The MVP is intentionally narrow: tell users their train is delayed or cancelled, and tell them what to do next. That's it.

The MVP in one sentence
When a Seatfrog user's train is delayed or cancelled, the app and Live Activity update immediately — and tell them clearly what their next steps are.
  • 🔔

    Push notification on disruption

    The moment a user's booked train is flagged as delayed or cancelled, they get a push. Not a generic alert — a personal one. "Your 14:30 London → Edinburgh is currently running 18 minutes late." They hear it from us before they hear it from anyone else.

  • 📋

    Clear "what do I do now?" next steps

    Tapping the notification opens a simple, calm screen: what's happened, what it means for their ticket, and what their options are. No jargon. No hunting. Just: "Your ticket is valid on the next available service. You don't need to do anything." Or: "This train is cancelled — here's how to travel or get a refund."

  • 🟢

    Live Activity status pill

    For users with an upcoming booked trip, a minimal Live Activity appears on the lock screen and Dynamic Island: the train status (On time / Delayed Xmins / Cancelled) and a single next-step prompt. No platform info yet, no connection tracking — just the status and what it means. One glance = enough information to act.

  • ✅

    Ticket validity reassurance

    The single highest-anxiety question when a train is cancelled is: "is my upgrade still valid?" The MVP answers this explicitly and immediately. "Your First Class upgrade is valid on the next available service." This one line, delivered at the right moment, is the difference between a 1★ review and a 5★.

Why start here

This MVP requires no new complex integrations beyond a reliable live train data feed (which we need to fix regardless). It doesn't need TrainSwap deep-linking, Delay Repay automation, or connection tracking. It just needs: know the user's trip → watch the train status → alert them proactively → tell them what to do. Ship this, measure it, then build the rest on proven foundations.

4 The Full Vision: What We Build Next

Once the MVP is live and the data feed is reliable, we expand the Live Activity into a full travel companion. Each phase adds a new layer of value — but the MVP alone is already a significant improvement over today.

  • 🏟️

    Platform Information

    When the platform is confirmed, it appears immediately on the Live Activity and Dynamic Island. No more hunting between apps. For connections, shows platform for each leg.

  • 🔄

    Disruption → TrainSwap Flow

    One-tap access to TrainSwap directly from the Live Activity when disruption is detected. "Your train is cancelled — swap for free?" Converts a pain point into a product moment that showcases Seatfrog's unique capabilities.

  • 🕐

    Connection Confidence

    For multi-leg journeys: real-time connection buffer. "Your connection at Birmingham is safe — 14 mins buffer." Reduces the single most anxiety-inducing moment of a complex journey.

  • 💰

    Delay Repay Assist

    When a qualifying delay occurs, Seatfrog proactively surfaces the claim: "Your train arrived 32 minutes late. You're owed 50% back. Shall I start the claim?" Turns £80M in unclaimed compensation into a loyalty moment.

8 Risks & Mitigations

Risk
Severity
Mitigation
Live data feed quality Medium
Showing wrong status (e.g. "on time" for cancelled) already damages trust — this must be solved before launch, not after.
Audit and replace the existing status data feed first. Don't launch Live Activity on unreliable data — it will make things worse.
Notification anxiety Medium
Our research already notes that current notifications add anxiety rather than reduce it. A badly-timed disruption push could worsen this.
Tiered notification design: critical travel alerts (always on, separated from marketing). User testing before launch. Only send alerts with actionable resolution, not just bad news.
iOS-first creates Android gap Low (plan for it)
Live Activities are iOS-only. Android users would get a worse experience.
Design Android equivalent using persistent rich notifications (already spec'd in roadmap). Must ship within same release cycle — not as an afterthought.
Scope creep / distraction from P1 fixes High (manage closely)
We still have payment/auction stability as P1. Engineering bandwidth is finite.
This is a Phase 2 initiative, contingent on payment/auction stability being resolved first. Don't start discovery on this until P1 fixes are in flight.

9 Recommendation

The evidence case for this is unusually strong. Multiple independent data sources — industry statistics, app store reviews, our own user survey, Reddit discussions, and competitive signals — all point to the same gap. We are currently abandoning users at their most anxious moment. Our CS team is world-class at fixing this after the fact (90% Trustpilot), but the self-serve experience is broken. This is the feature that closes that gap and turns a trust-destroyer into a trust-builder.

Start with the MVP. Ship the simplest possible version first: detect disruption, push a notification, tell the user what to do next, show it on a Live Activity. This alone will move the needle on reviews and retention — and prove the data feed works before we build more on top of it.
